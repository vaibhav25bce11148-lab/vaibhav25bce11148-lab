# Case Study: The "QR Code / Receive Money" UPI Scam

**What it is:** UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud often involves social engineering, where scammers trick victims into authorizing a payment under the false pretense of sending them money. 

**The Scenario:** Rahul, a second-year college student, posted an ad on an online marketplace to sell his old study table for ₹2,500. Within an hour, he received a call from a "buyer" who agreed to the price without negotiation.

**Step-by-Step Execution:**
1. The scammer claimed he was out of town but wanted to lock in the purchase by sending the money immediately via UPI.
2. To build trust, the scammer sent a test transaction of ₹1 to Rahul's account, which was successful.
3. Next, the scammer sent a QR code (and a "collect request") to Rahul on WhatsApp, instructing him to scan it and enter his UPI PIN to "receive" the remaining ₹2,499.
4. Unaware that entering a UPI PIN is *only* required for sending money, Rahul scanned the code and entered his PIN.
5. Instantly, ₹2,499 was deducted from Rahul's account. The scammer immediately blocked his number.

**Who is Targeted:** College students, the elderly, and frequent users of second-hand marketplaces (like OLX or Quikr) are prime targets because they are often eager to close a quick sale and might overlook security warnings.

**Consequences:** Victims suffer immediate financial loss and emotional distress. Because the user actively authorized the transaction by entering their PIN, recovering the funds through the bank can be a lengthy, difficult, and sometimes impossible process.
