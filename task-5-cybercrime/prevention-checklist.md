# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: Cybersecurity Checklist for College Students

Here are 8 actionable tips to protect your digital identity and finances:

1. **Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money.** Remember the golden rule: UPI PINs are only used to *send* money or check your balance. *(Financial/UPI Safety)*
2. **Do not scan random QR codes.** If someone sends you a QR code on WhatsApp or a marketplace app to "send you a payment," it is a scam. *(Financial/UPI Safety)*
3. **Use strong, unique passwords.** Avoid using the same password for your university portal, social media, and banking apps. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
4. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).** Turn on 2FA for your email, social media, and financial apps for an extra layer of security.
5. **Beware of public Wi-Fi.** Avoid logging into sensitive portals (like bank accounts or college fee payment gateways) using public campus or cafe Wi-Fi unless you are using a secure VPN.
6. **Verify urgent requests.** If a friend or family member suddenly messages you on social media or WhatsApp asking for urgent financial help, call them directly to verify their identity before sending anything.
7. **Keep software updated.** Regularly update your laptop and smartphone operating systems to patch known security vulnerabilities.
8. **Beware of fake internships.** Be highly cautious of "too good to be true" part-time jobs or internship offers that ask you to pay a "registration fee" or "equipment fee" upfront.

🚨 **REPORTING CYBERCRIME IN INDIA** 🚨
If you or someone you know falls victim to a cybercrime or financial fraud, report it immediately:
* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [www.cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Helpline Number:** Dial **1930** (Call immediately if money is deducted to freeze the transaction).
